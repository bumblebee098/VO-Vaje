# Policy Pack (Minimum Viable Set)

## 1. Access Control Policy

- Access to patient medical records, laboratory data, financial records, and HR data is restricted to authorized personnel only.
- All users must have unique accounts. Shared accounts are prohibited for telemedicine, clinical, and administrative systems.
- Access rights are granted based on role and least privilege and are reviewed at least quarterly.
- Strong password requirements and multi-factor authentication are enforced for all remote and cloud access.
- User access is revoked when employees leave or change roles.

## 2. Backup & Recovery Policy

- Daily backups of: critical systems and data, including patient records, telemedicine data, and financial records.
- Backups are stored both locally on the NAS, and offsite in a secure location.
- Quarterly tests of backup restoration to ensure recoverability.
- Encryption of backup media, access to backup storage is limited to authorized IT staff.
- Restoration procedures are documented and updated after each test or incident.

## 3. Incident Response Policy

- All security incidents must be reported immediately to the IT Manager and senior management.
- An incident response team is defined, including IT Manager, Security Officer, and Operations Lead.
- Incident handling follows the steps: identify, contain, eradicate, recover, and learn.
- Communications are coordinated with legal, compliance, and management before external disclosure.
- Post-incident review and lessons learned are documented and used to improve controls.

## 4. Vendor Security Policy

- Vendors handling patient or financial data must comply with GDPR and applicable healthcare regulations.
- Security requirements and breach notification clauses are included in all vendor contracts.
- Vendor security performance is reviewed at least annually, and third-party assessments are requested when needed.
- Access to vendor-provided systems is controlled, and vendor accounts are removed when services end.
- Critical SaaS providers are monitored for security incidents and service availability.

## 5. Acceptable Use Policy

- Company devices and systems are used only for approved business purposes.
- Personal software, unauthorized cloud storage, and unapproved external devices are not permitted on clinical systems.
- Users must not copy or transmit sensitive patient data outside approved systems.
- Suspicious emails, links, or system behavior must be reported immediately.
- Compliance with this policy is monitored and violations may result in disciplinary action.
